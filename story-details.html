कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन।
मा कर्मफलहेतुर्भूर्मा ते सङ्गोऽस्त्वकर्मणि॥

कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन।

मा कर्मफलहेतुर्भूर्मा ते सङ्गोऽस्त्वकर्मणि॥

20 April, 2023

This shloka from Bhagvat Gita which asks everyone of us to do to our duty to the best of our ability without worrying about the results is the crux of life story of Devshankar Singh an Upaz farmer from village Khagni of East Champaran .

52 year old Devshankar got nostalgic during the talk and told how he used to be the naughtiest child in the household and ignored his studies .He vividly remembers how his sister- in-law (wife of his elder brother ) used to treat him as his own child and gently persuaded him to focus on learning . That became a turning point in his life and then onwards he started valuing the importance of knowledge and using wisdom to achieve success in one's life . Later on he completed his graduation and worked for few years as an accountant in Calcutta.But due to interest in farming he returned to his village Khagni in Purbi Champaran and started providing knowledge of Agriculture as a Master Trainer in Government Schools . He says that "Fields gently reminds farmer to fulfil their needs in the form of fertilizer ,seeds and water and in return the field would take care of the farmer needs by giving then excellent yield ". Service of the community where he lives is the work he enjoys the most .

Praising her wife Kaushalya Devi, Devshankar says " she regularly guides and motivates other smallhold farmers in the village to focus on work and enjoy small farming activities ." His son Ankesh Kumar posted as Assistant Professor at Radharaman Group of Institutions in Bhopal ensures that his parents face no financial hardships and visits them regularly. Apart from farming Devshankar loves to travel had been to many places including the bordering country Nepal ,Indian citizens need not have a visa to enter Nepal.

According to his estimates crop yields have increased by more than 30 percent after he started taking services of UPAZ. He owns around four acre of agricultural land where he systematically grows paddy and other crops by consulting UPAZ field officers. UPAZ is enabling farmers of the country to focus on core of the farming while providing door step delivery of all essential and subsidiary requirements
